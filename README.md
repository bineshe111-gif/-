Project Overview
Targeting safety demands of unmanned wireless charging parking lots, this project develops a multi-module coordinated metal foreign object detection and removal system with master-slave robot collaboration, consisting of four modules: charging zone monitoring, RDKs100 master vehicle navigation, RDKx5 slave vehicle cleaning and millimeter-wave radar monitoring.
The STM32F407-based symmetrical coil monitoring unit detects metal debris and sends trigger signals to robots. The RDKs100 master vehicle navigates autonomously to deliver the RDKx5 slave vehicle. The slave adopts YOLO for foreign object visual positioning and transmits images to the master, which leverages large models to identify metal debris and guide the slave to remove contaminants via electromagnetic adsorption. The millimeter-wave radar identifies approaching creatures and cuts off charging power to avoid magnetic hazards, restoring power after living beings leave.

System Introduction
1. Wireless Charging Device
Given the 10–25 cm chassis height of real vehicles, a simulated charging device based on STM32F407 is built with dimensions 35 cm × 25 cm × 17 cm. Transmitting and receiving coils are sandwiched between two acrylic plates. An LED connected to the receiving coil lights up to indicate normal charging status.
2. Metal Debris & Living Body Detection Device
Reversely connected symmetrical coils conduct preliminary metal detection, while HLR12 millimeter-wave radar monitors nearby living creatures.
Alternating magnetic fields during charging generate eddy currents and reverse magnetic fields on tiny metal fragments. The coils sense induced voltage, whose differential signals are rectified and amplified. Charging pauses once the voltage difference exceeds the threshold; LoRa modules send alerts to the cleaning robot system, and charging resumes after clearance.
The millimeter-wave radar measures the distance and velocity of moving objects. Creatures exhibit continuous speed changes, unlike inanimate objects with abrupt velocity shifts. Combined distance and speed data are used to judge biological interference. The system triggers alarms and reduces charging power upon risks, recovering original power after a period with no subsequent alarms.
3. Master-Slave Foreign Object Cleaning Robot
A custom master-slave robot is developed based on Originbot and RDKs100. The master vehicle is equipped with RDKs100 and STM32, and the slave vehicle adopts RDKx5 (Figure 6).
Slave Vehicle
The two-wheel differential drive slave robot based on inverted pendulum theory adjusts its center of gravity dynamically. It can rotate in place for flexible operation in low, narrow spaces with simplified low-height mechanical structures. An electromagnetic absorber is installed to capture ferromagnetic metals. After YOLO visual recognition, the relay energizes the electromagnet to carry metal debris away from charging zones.
Master Vehicle
The four-wheel master chassis features low gravity, high stability and strong load capacity for full-area autonomous navigation on complex parking lot surfaces. A motor-driven lifting ramp gate enables slave vehicle deployment and storage. Equipped with LiDAR, the master robot builds high-precision global maps via SLAM when deployed to new sites, marking parking spaces, base stations and charging zone boundaries and storing map data on the on-board processor.
Detection & Cleaning Workflow
After new energy vehicles park, the coil sensor scans the charging zone. If metal debris is detected, charging stops and LoRa transmits alerts to the master robot. The master travels to the target parking spot at 5 cm/s and lowers the ramp for the slave to capture on-site images. YOLO crops foreign object areas and sends visual data back. The master’s large model confirms metal fragments and directs the slave to complete removal. The slave returns to the master after cleaning, and the master drives back to the base station.
