Focusing on safety requirements of unmanned wireless charging parking lots, this work develops a multi-module metal foreign object detection and removal system with master-slave vehicle coordination. It consists of four modules: charging area monitoring, RDKs100 master vehicle navigation, RDKx5 slave vehicle removal and millimeter-wave radar monitoring.The STM32F407-based symmetrical coil module detects metal foreign objects and sends trigger signals. The autonomously navigated RDKs100 master vehicle transports the RDKx5 slave vehicle to target areas. The slave vehicle uses YOLO for foreign object positioning and transmits images to the master vehicle, which identifies metal via large models; the slave vehicle then adsorbs and removes metallic debris.The millimeter-wave radar identifies approaching objects. It cuts off charging power when living beings draw near to prevent magnetic hazards and resumes charging after they leave.
